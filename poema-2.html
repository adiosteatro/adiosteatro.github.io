Un verano que no sabe irse

Diario 2023.06.13 · Dreamless Night

It has been a long time since we parted, yet you rarely visit me in dreams. I never understood why— why it was so hard to dream of you even when every waking hour was filled with you, even when your name hovered in my mind like the afterimage of a light that refused to fade.

One evening, I lifted my head toward the sky: a pale blue dissolving into night, a hush settling over me so tenderly that it felt like all the small attempts in my life had quietly failed without leaving a trace.

The rhythm of that summer was blurred and humid, like a sentence missing its punctuation— its heat breathing against my skin, my back damp with sweat that turned to mist under the streetlamps. And still, somehow, it reminded me of you.

“Why do we remember beautiful lies? We end up regretting them most of our lives.”

Perhaps I stopped dreaming because dreams were the one place where the truth waited without mercy— a place where I could not pretend that what happened between us was irreversible, or that I had already learned to accept it.

To dream of you would mean facing the goodbye I never wanted to say.

And the heaviness of summer— that intimate, clinging warmth— felt exactly like the adhesive ache left on my skin after tearing myself away from you.

A wound that healed slowly, and clung to everything before it finally let go.

Es sólo un recuerdo, pero insiste en quedarse un poco más.
